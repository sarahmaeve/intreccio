The founding of Rome

The histoire of l'Empire romain begins with a small ville on the banks of le fleuve Tibre — a ville the Romains called Roma. According to la légende, it was founded en 753 avant Jésus-Christ by two frères, Romulus and Remus. For plusieurs siècles, Rome was a république, governed by un sénat and two elected consuls. Then, en 27 avant J.-C., Octavien — better known as Augusto — became le premier empereur, and la république transformed into un empire. At its greatest extent, this empire stretched from la Grande-Bretagne to l'Egitto, from l'Espagne to la Syrie. La lingua of l'empire was le latin, and from le latin would eventually descend l'italiano, le français, l'espagnol, and les autres langues romanes.

Daily life and the fall

La vita quotidiana dans l'Empire romain was remarkably sophistiquée pour son époque. Les Romains built grandi strade (les grandes routes) that connected toutes les provinces of l'empire — tutte le strade portano a Roma, as the famous proverbe says. Ils construisaient aussi acquedotti (les aqueducs) to bring l'acqua to les villes, des thermes publics where i cittadini could bathe and socialize, et de grands amphithéâtres like il Colosseo à Rome, where les gladiateurs fought before des milliers de spectateurs. La famiglia romana was governed by le pater familias, le père, who had une autorité almost absolute over sa femme, ses enfants, et ses schiavi (les esclaves). Après quasi cinque secoli, l'empire d'Occident fell en 476 après J.-C., but son héritage — le droit, la lingua, l'architecture, la religione — continue de vivre in Italia et partout en Europe.
